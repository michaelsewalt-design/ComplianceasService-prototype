Step 4
Critical & Important Functions
Answer the following questions to determine whether this arrangement involves a Critical or Important Function (CIF) as defined under EBA GL/2019/02 Art. 8 and DORA Art. 3(22).
Important: A function is Critical or Important if its disruption would materially impair the financial performance of the institution, or the soundness or continuity of its regulated activities and services. Even partial outsourcing of such functions triggers enhanced requirements.
🔍
CIF Determination Questions
Would failure or disruption of this service materially impair the institution's ability to provide regulated financial services to clients?
Does the service account for a significant share of the institution's operational costs, revenues, or total assets managed?
Can this service be quickly replaced or performed in-house if the vendor relationship is terminated without notice?
Would disruption of this service result in regulatory breaches, reporting failures, or inability to meet prudential requirements?
Would a failure of this service cause significant reputational damage or loss of client trust?
Does the vendor have access to, or process, sensitive client data, payment data, or proprietary trading information?
Is this the same vendor (or its group) relied upon for multiple critical services, or does the same vendor serve a significant portion of the EU financial sector?

Institution Type & Applicable Framework
DORA applies to credit institutions, investment firms, payment institutions, e-money institutions, crypto-asset service providers, insurance undertakings and others listed in Art. 2 DORA.
ICT services are defined broadly in DORA Art. 3(21) — including software, hardware, data, cloud services, and IT-related support.
EBA Outsourcing GL apply to credit institutions and investment firms under CRD IV/CRR. From January 2025, overlapping provisions are streamlined with DORA.
